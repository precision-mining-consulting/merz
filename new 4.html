{/* Stoch Tool */}

Stoch - Stochastic Schedule Analysis

Purpose:

Runs 100+ variations of the deterministic schedule to quantify uncertainty and establish confidence ranges for production forecasts

Input Distributions:
  • • Availability: Equipment uptime variance
  • • Utilization: Operational efficiency ranges
  • • Rate: Dig rate variations by geology/conditions
  • • Delays: Weather, maintenance, operational stoppages
  • • Black Swan Events: Major unplanned disruptions
Output Analysis:
  • • P10/P50/P90 production curves
  • • Risk-adjusted schedule outcomes
  • • Sensitivity rankings
  • • Probability of meeting targets
  • • Critical path identification
When to Run:
  • • Annual plan development
  • • Major schedule changes
  • • Budget/guidance setting
  • • Risk assessment reviews
Integration Point:

Stoch runs after deterministic schedule creation but before final plan approval, providing management with confidence ranges and risk quantification

{/* Blandr Tool */}

Blandr - Linear Product Optimization

Purpose:

Runs linear programming optimization to determine optimal product blend strategy from available coal sources (pit uncovery + stockpiles) to maximize value

Input Parameters:
  • • Coal Uncovery: Timing and quality from mine schedule
  • • Stockpile Inventory: Available quantity and quality by time bucket
  • • Product Specifications: Customer quality requirements
  • • Pricing: Revenue by product grade
  • • Costs: Handling, processing, blending costs
Optimization Outputs:
  • • Optimal product selection mix
  • • Blending ratios (pit:stockpile)
  • • Margin maximization strategy
  • • Stockpile drawdown schedule
  • • Constraint violation warnings
Constraints Applied:
  • • CHPP capacity limits
  • • Stockpile max residency times
  • • Quality specification ranges
  • • Sales contract commitments
  • • Rail/port capacity windows
Integration Point:

Blandr runs after schedule completion to optimize product determination strategy, feeding results back into operational routing decisions and financial forecasts

{/* Tool Workflow */}

Schedule Tool Workflow

1. Deterministic Schedule
→
2. Stoch Analysis
→
3. Blandr Optimization
→
4. Final Plan
Step 1:

Create base case schedule using planned dig rates, equipment availability, and constraints

Step 2:

Run Stoch to quantify risks and establish P10/P50/P90 scenarios with probability distributions

Step 3:

Run Blandr on each scenario to optimize product mix and maximize margin given constraints

Step 4:

Select optimal strategy balancing value, risk tolerance, and operational feasibility

Key Consideration:

Stoch provides the "what could happen" while Blandr determines "what should we do". Both tools together enable risk-aware, value-optimized planning.
